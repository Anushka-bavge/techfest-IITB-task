Epipelagic Zone

The Sunlight Zone

Here, the ocean is bathed in warmth and light. Photosynthesis thrives, fueling 90% of all marine life. Waving meadows of seagrass and floating kelp capture the solar energy, sustaining dolphins, turtles, and schooling fish in a vibrant blue nursery.

200m - 1,000m

Mesopelagic Zone

The Twilight Zone

Light rapidly dissolves here into a cold, dim twilight. Photosynthesis is impossible. Creatures in this zone have evolved massive, sensitive eyes or tubular scopes to capture the last stray photons. It is home to the mysterious Giant Squid and schools of bristlemouths descending into the dark.

1,000m - 4,000m

Bathypelagic Zone

The Midnight Zone

Welcome to a realm of pitch-black darkness. The temperature hovers near freezing, and the pressure is crushing. Sunlight never reaches here. The only light is created by the inhabitants themselves—bioluminescence. Anglerfish dangle glowing lanterns to lure prey, while pulsing jellyfish flash neon warnings into the void.

4,000m - 6,000m

The Abyss

Abyssopelagic Zone

Derived from the Greek word meaning "bottomless," the Abyss contains over 80% of the flat ocean floor. Water pressure here climbs to 600 times that of the surface. Life relies on "marine snow"—a continuous shower of organic debris falling from above—or clustering around mineral-spewing hydrothermal vents.

6,000m - 11,000m

Hadal Zone

The Trenches

The ultimate frontier. Named after Hades, the Greek underworld, these deep V-shaped trenches are formed by tectonic plates colliding. In the Challenger Deep, pressure exceeds 1,100 atmospheres. Yet, in this absolute void, special amphipods, snailfish, and single-celled xenophyophores thrive, proving that life knows no bounds.
